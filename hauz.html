HAUZ KHAS

ABOUT HAUZ KHAS

Hauz Khas, a historic complex located in South Delhi, India, is a perfect blend of history, art, and modernity. Known for its medieval Islamic architecture, it features the remnants of a water reservoir, a mosque, and a tomb from the Khilji Dynasty. Today, Hauz Khas Village has become a vibrant hub of culture, nightlife, art galleries, and boutique stores.

The History Behind Hauz Khas

The term “Hauz Khas” translates to “Royal Tank.” It refers to the large water reservoir built by Alauddin Khilji in the 13th century to supply water to the residents of Siri Fort. Later, Feroz Shah Tughlaq renovated the reservoir and built a madrasa (Islamic seminary) and his tomb nearby, turning the site into a significant center of learning and culture.

Top Activities and Attractions

  • Explore Hauz Khas Fort: Wander through the ruins of the fort and enjoy stunning views of the lake.
  • Visit Art Galleries: Hauz Khas Village is home to several contemporary art galleries and exhibitions.
  • Experience Nightlife: Enjoy vibrant cafes, pubs, and bars that offer a lively nightlife experience.
  • Walk Around the Lake: Take a serene stroll around the Hauz Khas lake, surrounded by lush greenery.
